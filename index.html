Welcome



My project was dedicated to sports graphic art. I used photos off of the internet and edited them to look appealing to the general public. I had 20% of class time to do this but also worked on the project in my free time. It was often hard to be satisfied with a project, and I ended up with a lot of scrap projects. The "final exam" grade is based on building a webpage on top of the Bootstrap framework to show off my projects and the process of creating them.

PROJECTS

IN DEPTH

This is an in depth look at one of my projects. I created this on Adobe Photoshop. It's an edit of Connor McDavid, the captain of the Edmonton Oilers. He's one of my favorite players to make projects with because there's plenty of photos of him online, and I really like the how the Edmonton Oilers colors go with other things in nature. The first photo was just the one I found online. In the second one, I put McDavid on a cloudy sky background. I added orange and blue stripes behind him to match his jersey, and also added a motion blur effect and some more layers to his body. To add to the background, I wrote Connor McDavid in a text layer, then duplicated it to fill the screen, tilted it, and added a screen effect so it would blend into the background. In the third photo, I added some cloud PNG's I found online to unite the foreground with the background. The last photo was inspired by one of my older designs and added a pair of angel wings onto McDavid. This gives the edit more of a heavenly effect, which plays on a nickname given to McDavid by many Oilers fans: "McJesus".

MY PROCESS

These slides show my LAUNCH project process.

  • L: Look, Listen, and Learn. In this stage of the project, I researched the path that I was going to be taking. I found some information on other sports graphic creators and also looked into what kind of content worked best. I also came up with some questions I wanted to answer.
  • A: Ask Tons of Questions. Here, I came up with and answered more questions that I had. While I couldn't answer all of them, the things I found helped me progress with my project.
  • U: Understanding the Process. Here, I planned what my project would be and how the path I will take would go.
  • N: Navigate Ideas. I planned out specific points of my journey that I would visit, and what I wanted to do by the end of it.
  • C: Create a Prototype. This part was easy, I just used my design skills to create projects that I liked.
  • H: Highlight and Fix. This is where I asked Risinger for help on specific elements of my project.
